I am a postdoctoral researcher in Health Economics at Aix-Marseille School of Economics (Aix-Marseille Université).

My research sits at the intersection of infectious-disease prevention, health behaviors, and economic evaluation. I study how people adopt (or reject) preventive and diagnostic measures (vaccination policies, screening/testing strategies, and other countermeasures) and how these choices translate into outcomes that matter for public health and policy.

I work with stated-preference approaches (discrete choice experiments, best–worst scaling, and participatory value evaluation) and choice models. Currently, I contribute to projects on improving the acceptability and accessibility of countermeasures against emerging epidemic threats (ACME, Institut Pasteur).

Alongside this behavioral work, I also develop and contribute to HEOR-style evaluations, including cost and cost-effectiveness analyses, to support decisions about prevention and preparedness strategies, especially for infectious diseases. A key focus of my work is the prevention of climate-related infectious-disease outbreaks.

If you’d like to discuss or if you have any question, please feel free to reach out!
